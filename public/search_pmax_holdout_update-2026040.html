Geo incrementality · Google Search & PMAX holdout

Search & PMAX is revenue-positive — but most of the "savings" never materialize

A live, interactive readout of the 2026-04-20 weekly refresh. 53 lit DMAs, 50 dark DMAs. When Search & PMAX goes dark, Facebook, Affiliates, and Demand Gen rush to fill the gap — so the right question isn't "what do S&P cost?" but "does having them on make total ad spend more efficient?"

Eric's Executive TL;DR
Claude Overview Matched markets shows +4.73% order lift and +12.32% revenue lift in the lit cell, both at effectively 100% confidence. Revenue-model iROAS on S&P spend is 1.34x GP (≈2.67x top-line), close to the PMAX benchmark. Order lift now cleanly clears the 80% confidence bar (up from 77% in the 4/13 run).
⚠ Channel backfill — the real headline When Search & PMAX went dark in the control DMAs, spend didn't disappear — it got reallocated. Facebook spent $87k more, Affiliates $22k more, and Demand Gen $15k more in the dark cell. That absorbed ~91% of the S&P savings. The business-wide spend delta was only $7.7k. We are not actually saving ad spend by turning S&P off. The right framing: does having S&P on make our total ad spend more efficient? On total-business spend, iROAS is 23.5x GP — the denominator is too small to trust, but the directional answer is clearly yes.
Recommendation: keep Search & PMAX at current nationwide spend. Net cost is near zero after backfill; incremental revenue is conclusive. Next: a branded vs. non-branded Search holdout, and a PMAX-only holdout.
Layer 1 · Executive view
Signature view · what counts as "incremental spend"?

Orders iROAS + Revenue iROAS — toggle the denominator

The usual iROAS denominator is ΔSearch & PMAX spend ($135.6k). But because other channels backfilled 91% of that spend into the dark cell, the business only saw Δtotal ad spend of $7.7k. Toggle below to see how dramatically the read changes. This is the crux of the holdout: on S&P spend, revenue iROAS is 1.34x (below our 1.4x floor on GP basis); on total spend, revenue iROAS is 23.5x (denominator small enough to be unreliable, but the shape is clear).
Show the math · why the two denominators disagree by ~18x

Δ S&P spend = (treatment S&P spend × 0.7217 SF) − (control S&P spend) = $135,599. This is what Search & PMAX "cost" in the lit cell after accounting for cell-size differences.

Δ Total ad spend = (treatment ALL-channel spend × 0.7217) − (control ALL-channel spend) = $7,709. This is the net change to the total advertising bill once Facebook/Affiliates/DG stepped in.

The gap — $135,599 − $7,709 = $127,890 — is the backfill: dollars that moved from S&P into other channels when S&P went dark. 91% of the S&P delta was absorbed this way.

What this means for iROAS:

  • iROAS on S&P spend is the answer to: "for each dollar we spend on S&P, how much incremental GP do we get?"
  • iROAS on Total spend is the answer to: "for each net-new advertising dollar (after backfill), how much incremental GP?"

Both are valid framings. The S&P-spend view is the one that compares apples-to-apples with other channels. The Total-spend view describes what the business actually "paid" to get the lift — but depends on whether you trust the other channels to stop backfilling if S&P turns on and off.

Layer 3 · Deep-dive diagnostics
Leading indicator to watch

LC ROAS multiplier → warning threshold

Last-click ROAS is a proxy for true incrementality between geo tests. Using this run's multiplier (0.926x revenue model), if S&P LC ROAS drops below 1.51x, true iROAS is projected to fall below the 1.4x GP floor. Current S&P LC ROAS is 1.44x — essentially at the threshold. This is a major shift from the 4/13 run's reported 0.64x multiplier (which was calculated from a non-reproducible LC ROAS of 1.86x); refreshed data confirms S&P sits close to parity with last-click, not far below it.
Show the math · how the warning threshold is derived

Step 1. Compute this test's LC-to-true multiplier: geo iROAS (revenue) ÷ S&P LC ROAS.

→ 1.337 ÷ 1.443 = 0.926x. S&P is worth about 93¢ of true value for every dollar of LC ROAS.

Step 2. For a 1.4x true-iROAS floor: warn when LC ROAS × 0.926 < 1.4.

→ warn threshold (revenue model) = 1.4 ÷ 0.926 = 1.512x.

Current S&P LC ROAS = 1.443x. Buffer to threshold: ~5%. Watch this weekly.

Decision & next steps

What to do

Recommended
Keep Search & PMAX at current nationwide spend. Net cost after backfill is ~$8k; incremental GP revenue is ~$181k. Revenue lift is conclusive; order lift has cleared the 80% confidence bar. No change needed to S&P budget.
Watch
Combined S&P LC ROAS. Current: 1.44x GP. Warn threshold (revenue model): 1.51x. Buffer is thin. If LC ROAS drops below 1.30x GP, flag for re-evaluation.
Follow-up test #1 (priority)
Branded vs. non-branded Search holdout. The blended S&P iROAS sits between Stella's branded (0.70x) and PMAX (2.98x) benchmarks. Isolating branded search would clarify whether we are overpaying for branded Search. If branded is dragging blended iROAS down, we could reallocate.
Follow-up test #2
PMAX-only holdout. PMAX last-click GPM ($80k delta) and Stella's 2.98x PMAX benchmark suggest PMAX is carrying most of the lift. A PMAX-only geo test would isolate the channel and avoid branded/non-branded noise.
Retest
Full S&P holdout retest in Q4 2026, 6+ weeks, ideally without mid-test promos. The April 1–5 activity added noise this run (though it doesn't appear to have biased the MM structure).
