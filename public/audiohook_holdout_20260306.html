Orders iROAS = (Incremental Orders x Control-cell AOV) / Incremental Spend ($55,468 pixel payouts). Revenue iROAS = Incremental GP Revenue / Incremental Spend. Revenue figures are already gross profit — no margin multiplier applied.

Critical context

Sensitivity: pre-offer vs. post-offer split

Around Feb 15, 4Patriots launched a $29 offer that compressed AOV from ~$398 to ~$291. Most test-period lift concentrated in the 22 days before that offer (+188 orders, +14.0%). GA4 data shows the post-offer window still produced positive lift (+84 orders, +6.5%), unlike the 1P analysis which showed ~zero. This suggests GA4's IP-based DMA resolution captures post-offer lift that billing-zip DMA assignment missed.
Show the math · what the offer change did

The $29 offer launched ~Feb 15 and dominated the product mix for the remainder of the test. Treatment AOV dropped from $398 pre-offer to $291 post-offer. Control AOV showed a similar drop ($392 to $291), so the shift was business-wide — not Audiohook-driven.

Unlike the 1P analysis (which showed ~zero post-offer lift), GA4 data shows +84 incremental orders (+6.5%) in the post-offer window. This suggests GA4's IP-based DMA resolution captures lift that billing-zip DMA assignment missed. Podcast audio ads still drove consideration even after the offer shift, though at a lower rate than pre-offer (+14.0%).

Data provenance

Methodology and data sources

  • Orders and revenue: sales_by_dma_by_channel_ga4.csv (GA4, IP-based DMA resolution). This is the primary source for all cross-channel and headline metrics.
  • Scale factor: 0.7719, derived from sales_by_dma.csv (GA4 aggregate) pre-period (Nov 30, 2025 – Jan 23, 2026). Applied universally to all metrics.
  • Spend denominator: Audiohook pixel payouts ($55,468). The pixel calculates payouts as CPA x assisted conversions x 65% default IF. This is the spend figure used in the primary iROAS calculation.
  • IO cap: $45,500 (billable amount per finance). After reaching this cap, the pixel continued recording payouts that were not charged. We use the full pixel payout total to model what ongoing performance would look like at pixel-reported rates, regardless of the IO cap.
  • iROAS at IO cap: Shown as secondary comparison. Blended Orders iROAS at IO = 2.35x (vs. 1.93x at pixel payouts).
Flags to watch

Methodological flags

  • ISSUE · Pre-period MAPE: 19.0% (above 15% cutoff). GA4 DMA resolution produces a structural cell-size imbalance (SF=0.77), resulting in higher MAPE than the 1P baseline. Results are somewhat directional as a consequence.
  • ISSUE · Revenue MAPE: 44.5%. Revenue comparisons are directional only.
  • WATCH · Revenue model divergence: blended 1.71x (pixel) vs orders 1.93x (pixel). Revenue model is lower in GA4 due to negative cooldown revenue delta.
  • ISSUE · Synthetic control collapsed to a single DMA (Raleigh-Durham NC). Excluded from all analysis.
  • WATCH · AOV collapse mid-test: $29 offer launched ~Feb 15, compressing AOV from ~$398 to ~$291. GA4 data still shows positive post-offer lift (+6.5%, 84 orders), unlike the 1P data which showed ~zero. This is a notable divergence between the two data sources.
  • WATCH · Iran news spike (Feb 27 – Mar 6) overlapped the highest assisted conversion week (196 AH conversions). May have inflated both treatment and control equally.
  • WATCH · Pixel continued recording payouts beyond the $45,500 IO cap. iROAS at IO cap ($45,500) is 2.35x — well above the 1.4x floor. Pixel payout total ($55,468) used for ongoing performance modeling.
Decision & next steps

What to do

Billing — adjusted IF
Apply the 55.6% incrementality factor (replaces Audiohook's default 65%). Recalculate CPA using: CPA = trailing AOV / 1.4x iROAS floor. Two scenarios:
• Pre-offer AOV ($392): CPA = $280
• Blended AOV ($226): CPA = $161
Recommend adjusting payout terms for renewal: test-derived IF (55.6%) + CPA based on trailing AOV / 1.4x target.
IO cap vs. pixel spend
The pixel continued recording beyond the $45,500 IO cap. iROAS using the IO cap ($45,500) is 2.35x — well above the 1.4x floor. iROAS at full pixel payouts ($55,468) is 1.93x — still clears. Use the pixel payout figure to model what ongoing performance looks like at pixel-reported rates, regardless of the IO cap.
AOV risk
CPA must track AOV. At $150 AOV → target CPA drops to $107. At $116 AOV → target CPA drops to $83. If the $29 offer stays dominant, podcast ads may not clear the 1.4x floor at lower AOVs. Monitor trailing 30-day AOV monthly.
Retest Q3 2026
Run a clean retest once AOV stabilizes. The $29 offer confounded the back half of this test. A Q3 2026 retest — after the offer either sunsets or becomes the new baseline — will confirm whether the 55.6% factor holds at steady-state AOV.
