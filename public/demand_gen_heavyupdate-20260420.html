Three-model iROAS comparison

Matched markets is the primary model. Synthetic control and whole-country are directional validators. When all three point the same direction and magnitude, confidence strengthens. Toggle the model (orders-based is primary) and the window below.
Show the math · how each bar is computed

Orders iROAS = (Incremental Orders × Control-cell AOV) ÷ Incremental Spend. Revenue iROAS = Incremental GP Revenue ÷ Incremental Spend. Revenue figures in our CSVs are already gross profit — no margin multiplier is applied.

Incremental orders = treatment orders − (control orders × scale factor). MM scale factor this run: 1.1890. The same orders-based SF is applied universally to orders, revenue, spend, and sessions — we do not compute per-metric SFs.

Spend, sessions, orders — week by week

DG spend and sessions move together with an elasticity near 1.0. Week 5 is the clearest example: national DG spend dropped ~41%, session lift dropped ~45%. Drag the scrubber to inspect any week.

DG spend vs. incremental sessions (weekly)

Show the math · elasticity and the Week 5 dose-response

Weekly inc DG spend and inc sessions have a Pearson correlation of r = +0.98 across the 6-week trimmed window. Elasticity estimated as ∂ln(sessions) / ∂ln(spend) ≈ ~1.0. In practical terms: a 40% cut in DG spend produced roughly a 40% cut in incremental sessions. Week 5 shows this cleanly — cut from ~$29k/day to ~$16k/day, session lift fell from +22% to +13%.

Show the math · how each channel's revenue delta is computed

Orders Model (per channel): inc_revenue = inc_orders × $169.04 (control-cell AOV, MM trimmed). Strips out AOV-mix noise — each channel's value is a pure order-volume translation.

Revenue Model (per channel): inc_revenue = observed last-click GP revenue in treatment − (last-click GP revenue in control × 1.189 SF). Reflects what GA4 actually recorded, AOV differences included.

Source: sales_by_dma_by_channel_ga4.csv (GA4, IP-based DMA resolution — same basis as the matched-markets headline), trimmed window, all DMAs in test. When the two models disagree sharply on a channel, it signals an AOV shift — e.g., the treatment channel pulled in higher- or lower-ticket orders than its order-count delta suggests.

Layer 3 · Deep-dive diagnostics
Leading indicator to watch

LC ROAS multiplier → warning threshold

Last-click DG ROAS is a proxy for true incrementality — we don't get a fresh geo test every week. Using this run's LC-to-true multiplier, the BAU warning threshold for DG LC ROAS is 0.36x, and the heavy-up-level threshold is 0.49x. Current DG LC ROAS in treatment sits at 0.52x (GA4). If LC ROAS drops below the heavy-up threshold, pause the heavy-up until the next geo readout.
Show the math · how the warning threshold is derived

Step 1. Compute this test's LC-to-true multiplier: true iROAS ÷ DG LC ROAS (treatment).

Step 2. For a 1.4x true-iROAS floor: warn when DG LC ROAS × multiplier < 1.4x.

→ warn threshold (orders model) = 1.4 ÷ 3.16 = 0.44x.

For BAU (no heavy-up), the multiplier is higher because BAU LC ROAS is typically higher relative to true iROAS — so the BAU warn threshold of 0.40x is slightly lower.
