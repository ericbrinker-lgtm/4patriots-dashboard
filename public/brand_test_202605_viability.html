0.What is this test?

Two parallel brand-video geo tests, measured against matched control DMAs, to isolate the full-bundle effect from the 3-channel effect.

We stand up two brand video campaigns at the same time. The first runs the full bundle — Cox TV + YouTube + Facebook + CTV — in the 20 Cox-deliverable DMAs. The second runs a 3-channel bundle — YouTube + Facebook + CTV only, no TV — in a matched set of 12 non-Cox DMAs chosen to cover regional gaps in the Cox footprint (Northeast, Midwest, Pacific NW).

Measuring both in parallel does three things at once: it tests whether brand video works in our mix, it tests whether it works without Cox TV in markets Cox can't reach, and it tells us how much of the full-bundle effect is specifically Cox TV. The answer drives the national rollout decision.

What this strategy should deliver

  • Reach more of our 55+ homeowner target outside our normal digital reach. Linear TV indexes heavily on 55+ homeowners; YouTube and CTV also reach this demo but less concentrated.
  • Improve conversion rates and order volume over time. Brand-built audiences convert at higher rates on DR channels (Binet & Field / IPA).
  • Reduce price sensitivity. Pricing power is a classic outcome of sustained brand investment.
  • Increase share of market. Les Binet's SOV/SOM research: brand spend above category SOV grows SOM over 12–24 months.
  • Increase organic / untracked orders. Brand lift shows up as direct traffic, organic search, and phone orders that last-click can't credit back.
⏱
The major ask here is time, not money. Cox+ reads at 6 months. YT/FB/CTV needs 12 months because brand-only channels accumulate effect more slowly and the 3-channel cell has a higher statistical noise floor. Plan for a staggered read.

1.Spend plan and what it buys

$4,000/day total test spend — $15,688/day national-equivalent when prorated to a national rollout. Fits the $10-20k/day target.

The recommended spend is asymmetric across cells because the cells have different noise profiles. The Cox+ cell is cleaner (σ=7.9 orders/day) so each dollar detects more. The YT/FB/CTV cell is noisier (σ=16.2 orders/day) so we need more spend to clear the detection floor — but even at higher spend, the 6-month MDE stays above realistic brand-decay levels. That's the reason YT/FB/CTV primary adjudication is deferred to month 12.

2.When do we detect each cell?

Running signal-to-noise ratio over the measurement window, at the recommended spend, for both cells.

80% confidence (S/N ≥ 1.28) is our directional floor. 95% confidence (S/N ≥ 1.96) is a conclusive read. The Cox+ cell crosses both thresholds well inside the 6-month cooldown. The YT/FB/CTV cell crosses 80% around month 9 and 95% between month 12 and 15 — which is why YT/FB/CTV primary adjudication is deferred.

3.When does each cell pay back?

Cumulative iROAS over time. Brand video pays back slowly regardless of cell — the decay curve is the same.

The underlying brand-decay curve is the same for both cells (same category, same creative, same customer). What differs is when the effect becomes detectable through the statistical noise. Running iROAS climbs toward 1.0x by month 24 and toward the long-run 1.4x asymptote only past month 30.

Running (cumulative) iROAS under the Binet & Field / Nielsen blended decay curve assumption. Same curve for both cells. Green dashed: 1.4x floor (long-run target, not a 6-month bar).

Important framing for stakeholders: Brand video is not a 6-month investment. The right bar at M6 is "is Cox+ tracking the curve?" (≥0.77x period, ≥0.37x running). The right bar at M12 is "are both cells tracking?" (≥1.15x period, ≥0.70x running). The 1.4x floor is a month-30+ reality.

4.When is ramp-up over?

Period iROAS (monthly rate) vs. the long-run target. Ramp-up ends around month 18–24.

Period iROAS (each month standing alone). Crossing the 1.4x line = steady-state performance. Before that, this is a learning investment; after that, evaluate like any mature channel.

Ramp-up (months 1–12): Hold both cells to decay-curve milestones, not to the 1.4x floor. Cox+ gets adjudicated at M6. YT/FB/CTV waits to M12.
Steady state (month 18+): Hold both cells to 1.4x period iROAS. If either fails here, that cell's channel mix gets pulled back.

6.Warnings: how to do this wrong

Risks specific to the two-cell design plus the usual brand-video traps.
  • Calling YT/FB/CTV too early. The 6-month MDE on YT/FB/CTV is 1.41x. If we look at that cell at 6 months and see 0.4x, that's statistically meaningless. It doesn't mean the 3-channel bundle failed. Primary read is month 12.
  • Channel-level spend contamination across cells. If any non-test YouTube/Facebook/CTV spend lands in either cell's DMAs during the test, the 3-channel attribution gets muddy. Lock channel-level DMA allocations for 12 weeks, both cells.
  • Asymmetric promos. A July 4 promo concentrated in one cell but not the other will bias that cell's iROAS. Confirm with Marketing that promo distribution stays DMA-neutral across both Cox+ and YT/FB/CTV cells and their controls.
  • Wrong threshold. Do not hold ramp-up period iROAS to the 1.4x floor. Use decay-curve-adjusted milestones: M6 ≈ 0.77x (period), M12 ≈ 1.15x, M24 ≈ 1.39x.
  • Ignoring Whole Country on YT/FB/CTV. Unlike Cox+ WC (pre-period trend p=0.0007), the YT/FB/CTV WC has no significant trend (p=0.73). For the YT/FB/CTV cell, WC is a respectable validator, not just directional. Check it.
  • YT/FB/CTV CPM heterogeneity. Different CPMs across Boston vs Minneapolis vs Seattle could shift effective treatment intensity per DMA. Track delivered impressions by DMA to confirm homogeneous delivery.
  • Data gaps. Pre-period covers winter/spring only. Rerun match validation on 2026 Q2–Q3 data before launch if available.

8.Four scenarios and what to do about them

The two-cell design gives four possible outcomes. Each has a different rollout implication.
Case 1 — ideal

Both pass. Full bundle and 3-channel both clear decay curve.

National rollout viable across the whole country. Cox+ in Cox markets; YT/FB/CTV in non-Cox markets. The delta (Cox+ iROAS − YT/FB/CTV iROAS) tells us Cox TV's marginal value.

Case 2 — TV leads

Cox+ passes. YT/FB/CTV misses.

Cox TV is the lift mechanism. National rollout concentrates on Cox-deliverable markets. Retest YT/FB/CTV with different creative, daypart, or channel mix.

Case 3 — broken

Both miss floor.

Brand video concept doesn't work at current spend or creative. Stop, diagnose, iterate before retesting. This would be a meaningful negative result.

Case 4 — unusual

Cox+ misses. YT/FB/CTV passes.

Unlikely given 3-channel is a subset of full bundle. If observed, Cox TV may be cannibalizing or mismatched creative. Investigate before any Cox-heavy rollout.

Recommended rollout path if Case 1

  1. Q4 2026: Complete Cox+ 6-month cooldown read. Publish interim Cox+ adjudication.
  2. Q1–Q2 2027: Complete YT/FB/CTV 12-month cooldown read. Publish full two-cell adjudication.
  3. Q3 2027: National rollout. Full bundle in Cox markets (9.9%); 3-channel bundle in non-Cox markets (90.1%). Retain small regional holdouts (5-10 DMAs each) for ongoing measurement.
  4. Month 18 from original launch: Mid-test read to confirm brand effect is building toward steady-state.
  5. Month 24+: Move brand video into regular channel mix. Evaluate period iROAS against 1.4x floor quarterly.
