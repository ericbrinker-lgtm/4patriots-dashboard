Three-model iROAS comparison — DG's incremental return

Matched markets is the primary model — it anchors the headline. Synthetic control and whole-country are directional validators. Toggle the model (Orders is primary), the window, and the denominator. Read these bars as "the GP iROAS DG earned while running at BAU" — the test measures DG-on (control) vs. DG-off (treatment). Denominator: DG spend only. Conventional channel iROAS — divides DG's incremental GP by the DG spend delta alone. Halo revenue is in the numerator (incremental orders include cross-channel halo); halo spend is ignored in the denominator.
Show the math · how each bar is computed

Orders iROAS = (DG Incremental Orders × Control-cell AOV) ÷ DG Incremental Spend. DG incremental orders = control (DG-on) − treatment (DG-off), scaled. Revenue iROAS = DG Incremental GP Revenue ÷ DG Incremental Spend. Revenue in the CSVs is already gross profit — no margin multiplier is applied.

DG incremental spend = control DG spend × MM scale factor − treatment DG spend (treatment was near $0 during the test). This is the dollar difference DG was running at BAU vs. dark. The same orders-based SF is applied universally to orders, revenue, spend, and sessions. MM scale factor this run: 1.0199.

Cooldown window · interpreted with care

The 6-day cooldown window (Mar 1 – Mar 6)

DG resumed immediately on Mar 1 in the treatment cell — DG spend jumped from near-zero to ~$14.6k/week, back in line with control. So this isn't a true "channel-off cooldown." It's a post-resumption window, and DG's incremental contribution narrows to zero here as expected (DG is now running in both cells). Confidence is very low (15–16%) — don't read into it.
Why we include this card anyway

The test design included Mar 1 – Mar 6 as a cooldown, so we report it. But because DG spend returned to BAU on day 1 of the cooldown, there's no meaningful "channel-off residual" to measure. The combined window (Jan 10 – Mar 6) is the right reference when you want a 56-day roll-up — not the cooldown in isolation.

Procedure note: cooldown analysis is standard for TOF tests. This was a BOF/mid-funnel holdout, so even with a clean cooldown we'd expect small residuals. With DG back on immediately, the signal here is essentially zero.

Layer 3 · Deep-dive diagnostics
Incrementality factor · the headline of this validation

DG's incrementality factor on BAU LC ROAS

This is the answer the test was built to produce: for every $1 of DG last-click ROAS, DG delivers 2.51x of true incremental GP to the business. That multiplier reflects DG's halo through Facebook, MKT, Search, Unknown, and PMAX — revenue those channels get last-click credit for that DG actually seeded. Use this factor to translate weekly DG LC ROAS into a real-world iROAS read between geo tests. The warning threshold is 0.56x — if DG LC ROAS falls below that, the implied true iROAS is slipping under the 1.4x GP floor. Derived from the DG-only iROAS — use the denominator toggle in the hero chart above to see the all-ad version.
Show the math · how the warning threshold is derived

Step 1. LC ROAS (BAU) = control DG GA4 revenue ÷ control DG spend, test window. Reads: 0.60x.

Step 2. Multiplier (BAU) = geo-measured iROAS (MM Orders, test, DG spend only) ÷ LC ROAS (BAU) = 1.51x ÷ 0.60x = 2.51x.

Step 3. For a 1.4x GP iROAS floor: warn when DG LC ROAS × multiplier < 1.4x → warn threshold = 1.4 ÷ 2.51 = 0.56x.

Interpretation: every $1 of DG LC ROAS reported by Google is worth ~$2.5 in actual incremental GP to the business. That's because DG's halo across Facebook, MKT, Search, PMAX, and Unknown-attributed orders is larger than DG's direct last-click contribution. Without this test, DG's LC ROAS alone (0.60x BAU) would look sub-floor — the incrementality factor is what proves the channel is pulling its weight.

Flags to watch

New, resolved, and ongoing flags

  • DATA LIMIT · Pre-period only 41 days, not the 90 we'd prefer. sales_by_dma.csv starts 2025-11-30. Pre-period MAPE is 26% orders / 52% revenue (above our 10% / 20% targets). Daily correlation 0.98 rescues the match enough to read results, and test-period confidence 94% says signal broke through. Flag kept on the dashboard so future readers see the caveat.
  • COOLDOWN CAVEAT · DG spend resumed on Mar 1 (day 1 of the 6-day cooldown window). This is not a clean post-channel-off cooldown — it's a resumption window. Cooldown contribution narrows to zero and confidence is 16%. Report but don't over-interpret.
  • Orders vs. Revenue iROAS divergence · 1.51x (Orders) vs. 1.77x (Revenue) on the test window. Not a huge gap (<0.5x) but consistent — DG drove slightly higher-AOV orders than its order-count delta alone suggests. Report both.
  • Whole-country iROAS is materially softer · WC Orders = 1.06x, WC Revenue = 1.13x. Directionally agrees with MM and SC (DG is incremental) but magnitude is much lower. WC is known to be noisier with DMA-level heterogeneity — flag and lean on MM + SC for the headline.
  • DG clears the 1.4x floor · MM Orders 1.51x, MM Revenue 1.77x, SC Revenue 1.77x — three readings at or above the floor. MM Orders on the combined window (1.42x) also clears. DG is profitable at BAU spend.

DG's own direct LC contribution was only +$52k. The halo is ~4x the direct hit — matches the prior-run narrative and makes the 2.51x incrementality factor the right number to share externally.

  • ONGOING · Prior run landed near 2.2x (revenue-adjudicated). This run's MM Revenue (1.77x test / 1.93x combined) and SC Revenue (1.77x test / 2.23x combined) are within that neighborhood. MM Orders (1.51x) is lower — the gap is explained by which model you adjudicate on; all readings validate DG as incremental.
Decision & next steps

What to do

Recommended
Keep DG running at BAU. DG is validated as incremental — it cleared the 1.4x GP floor on the Orders headline (1.51x, 94% confidence), with the Revenue model and Synthetic Control corroborating. The cross-channel halo (Facebook, MKT, Search, Unknown, PMAX) is the bigger story than DG's direct last-click line — and it's what makes the 2.51x incrementality factor possible.
Watch
DG LC ROAS (GA4). Warning threshold: 0.56x (from DG-only iROAS). If weekly LC ROAS drops below this, true iROAS is approaching the 1.4x floor — pause to reassess or schedule a fresh geo read. Current BAU LC ROAS (control cell): 0.60x — close to the threshold, so monitor tightly.
Follow-up test
Re-validate DG in 1–2 quarters, or sooner if creative/placement mix shifts meaningfully. A partial-channel holdout (e.g., Gmail-only or Discover-only dark) could separate which placements carry the incrementality factor versus which are auction-side noise — sharpening where to lean in harder.
Data hygiene ask
Extend the historical window of sales_by_dma.csv so future pre-periods can cover the full 90 days. This run's 41-day pre-period forced us to work around a higher-MAPE match.
